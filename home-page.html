Project JP4S

Project Overview

The project is a fan-made one of the original game made by N called "Schoolgirl Supervisor - Saori Sato". Later additions of the series exist with "Schoolgirl Supervisor - Wildlife" and "JP Schoolgirl Supervisor Multi(player)". As such, the project has been named "JP4S", which stands for "Japanese Schoolgirl Supervisor - Saori Sato".
Project Technologies

The game is being developed in the Unity 3D Engine with C#. It currently employs the xNode plugin for the dialogue system. As for the 3D models, a combination of VRoid Studio and Blender is used. Currently, no sound system has been implemented. The game will not feature online-functionality, meaning that it can be played even with no connection.
Target Experience

The target audience is people 16 years or older. The game is NOT meant for children as it covers potentially heavy topics. There will be no nudity but there may be some gore content/content about death.
The user experience needs to be carefully considered when designing this game. As such, during its development, you should be able to see the following:
  • Compatibility
    • The products should be available for many PCs. This means that the performance should be carefully monitored and the game should be compiled for both Windows and GNU/Linux-based operating systems.
  • Accessibility
    • The products should be usable by many people, regardless of their capabilities. Things like visual impairment or auditory issues are going to be respected and considered.
  • Security
    • The products should not interfere with the safety of anyones devices and applications need to be tested properly.
  • Cohesion
    • The products will have a defining theme to aid in recognision.
  • Creativity
    • The game is free to be moddified under the condition that it is as freely available as the original game and does not cause any harm to anyones devices. (See the license on the official GitHub repository.)
(Please note, that products include both the game and the website in most cases.)
How do I uphold these guidelines?

Here are the five given guidelines and some examples for each:
  • How I incorporate compatibility
    • I would code in a way that avoids unnecessary computations and data requests. I would also compile and test the game for target platforms. As to why I cannot (as of now) develop for macOS: Apple is notorious for restricting their products to only other Apple users. Since I do not have a way (currently) of emulating macOS, I cannot test the game. Also compiling for macOS has always been a difficult thing for me to do due to not having Apple hardware, so unfortunately I cannot make the game accessible to macOS users.
  • How I work towards making things more accessible
    • A lot of smart people have come together and wrote a lot of guidelines for web developers to make their websites more accessible. As such, I am writing the website in a way that will enable people who use text-enlargement settings in their browser (like setting it to 150% for example), to perfectly view the content with ease. I also scaled the letter spacing up a bit to ensure that they don't merge together.
    Unfortunately the same cannot be said about game development, but I do research and incorporate a lot of my findings into the game. Stuff like the dialogue will have many customization options for me to display emotions even without any voice acting, but this comes at the cost of readability. Fear not, the text colors and animations will be completely optional! Other examples would be enabling people to remap key-bindings to acommodate their playstyle and perhaps even enabling the use of non-traditional controllers.
  • Security is also worth mentioning
    • In this day and age, many companies will try to gather as much info about their users as possible. This often comes at a cost: the freedom and safety of the users are sacreficed for profit. But do not trust these companies to actually protect your data either. Many data leaks have occured and will occur. As such, there will never be any requirement to log into an account or give up personal data to enjoy this project. Anything from the game itself to the website will be freely available. To my amazement: some companies enforce a strict always-online connection when playing their games, even if they are "offline", singleplayer games. You can be assured no such thing will be found here.
    The file management in the game will also always be contained within a singular folder and won't access other parts or your system either.
  • Unifying art and technology
    • Styling guidelines are written by me and I will do my best to uphold them. That way, the game and the website will both be recognisable to anyone and there won't be any strange changes made to it that do not fit the game. I am working very hard on ensuring both accessibility and aesthetics having a place. Because no one should to be forced to use a product that looks great but doesn't suit them well and neither should they be forced to make the product "uglier" for it to be usable. Everyone has the right to enjoy art and functionality.
    Video games are a form of art. They incorporate many things like visual arts, music, animation, storytelling and more. I think this view of video games should be more common in the industry and I will do my part in spreading this positive mindset.
  • Enabling creativity amongst developers
    • As you may have guessed, I do advocate for consumer rights and think that everyone should have the right to use a product they own however they want to, as long as it doesn't ruin the experience for others in an objective way. As such, I also encourage you to try learning things yourself and demonstrating your creativity with the tools that are available to you. If you would like to reinvent the story in your way, feel free to do so. But please pay attention to the following:
    1. Ensure that the game is accessible to everyone. Do not remove the accessibility features that I have already implemented just because you find it bothersome to work with.
    2. Make the game as free as the original. This means that if I am not making money from it, you cannot either. If my game is available to be modified, so does your game need to. And if I make my source code public, so do you.
    3. Do not harm anyone with your product. Harvesting data non-consentially, adding flashing lights without warning anyone about it, turning someones computer into a crypto-mining machine is uncool. Please be aware of your audience.
    4. Do not market the game towards children. This game IS NOT FOR KIDS. Even if your modification is all sunshine and flowers compared to mine, DO NOT MARKET IT TOWARDS KIDS. Of course this is hard to prevent fully, I know that because I was always interested in horror and war-themed games and media when I was a kid. Even though I handled these things well and maturely, others may not. Just do not intentionally market towards children. "Why?", you may ask. It's because it invites them into the community and the game as a whole, which may get them to play the original game too. Also all other communities that I have seen full of 13-year old kids have always had the same issue, a lot of pedophiles know that kids like these so they join these commmunities to try and groom kids. Think about all the stuff that goes on in communities like Roblox, Five Nights at Freddy's, Gacha Life and many more.
